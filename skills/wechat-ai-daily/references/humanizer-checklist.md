# Humanizer Checklist

Use this pass after drafting and before preview.

## Remove

- Chatbot prefaces: "当然", "下面是", "希望这能帮助你".
- Mechanical order words when they do not carry real structure: "首先", "其次", "最后", "综上所述".
- Grand claims: "重要意义", "深远影响", "标志着", "未来可期".
- Vague authority: "业内人士认为", "有报告显示" without naming the source type.
- Explicit persona labels: "我是一个务实、积极、乐观的人".
- Generic positive endings that could fit any AI article.
- Repeated three-part structures that make the rhythm sound assembled.
- Overuse of bold text, emoji, and decorative punctuation.

## Add

- One small concrete scene.
- One first-person judgment where it helps the reader understand why the topic matters.
- One limitation or tradeoff.
- One action the reader can try today.
- Plain source wording: "回到论文/官方文档/开源 README 确认".

## Read-Aloud Check

Read the article once as if explaining it to a colleague. Delete anything you would not actually say. Keep the warm tone, but let warmth come from care and clarity, not slogans.
